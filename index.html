How to interpret this tool

Surge is a downstream restriction—not a shortcut to the house

The model routes rainfall-generated runoff from the neighborhood into a ditch, through a canal and river, and finally into Lake Pontchartrain. As the lake and river rise, the hydraulic gradient shrinks. Drainage slows, stalls, or reverses, allowing locally generated rainwater to accumulate upstream.

The equations are intentionally simplified and use fictional geometry. The visualization is suitable for education and scenario comparison—not property-level prediction, design, insurance, evacuation, or regulatory decisions.

Drainage ∝ capacity × sign(ΔH) × √|ΔH| where ΔH is upstream water-surface elevation minus downstream water-surface elevation.
